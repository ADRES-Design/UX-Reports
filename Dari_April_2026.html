1. Executive Summary

A snapshot of the April 2026 helpdesk picture: where users are getting stuck, which services are taking the heaviest hit, and what the highest-impact moves look like.

How users are getting stuck

Top 5 services by ticket volume

The five things leadership should know

  1. Users want to update their own information. The two highest-volume issues are users emailing support to update personal details (email, phone, name) or company details (trade license, address). Together these are 20% of all tickets — clear demand for a self-service profile editor.
  2. When something breaks, users get no explanation. Generic "Something went wrong" errors, blank pages, and unrecoverable workflows account for almost 1 in 5 tickets. Adding clear error messages and reference codes would absorb most of this load.
  3. Service eligibility rules surface only after users fail. Abu Dhabi rules (auto-closure, off-plan, license validity) are correctly enforced but not disclosed upfront. Users invest time before discovering they cannot proceed.
  4. One in three "tickets" is operational noise. Auto-emails, internal threads, and misrouted helpdesk tags inflate volume metrics by ~30%. Filtering at intake would clean dashboards and free up support time.
  5. Backend integration is the highest-leverage investment. Data not syncing between Dari and other systems (ELMS, SmartHub, TAMM, banks) is numerically the smallest pain category but causes downstream failures across all the others.
